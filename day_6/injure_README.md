Assalam-o-Alaikum! Welcome to your academic vocabulary lesson. Today, we are learning the word family **"Injure."** This word is very important when talking about health, safety, and research data.

---

### 1. FULL WORD FAMILY

*   **Verb forms:**
    *   Injure
    *   Injures
    *   Injuring
    *   Injured (past tense/participle)
*   **Noun forms:**
    *   Injury
    *   Injuries (plural)
*   **Adjective forms:**
    *   Injured
    *   Uninjured
*   **Adverb forms:**
    *   None (not commonly used)

---

### 2. EXPLANATION

*   **Injure (Verb):** To hurt someone or something.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Kisi ko chot pahunchana ya nuqsan dena.
*   **Injures (Verb):** Third-person singular form.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Jab koi cheez kisi ko nuqsan deti hai.
*   **Injuring (Verb):** The act of hurting.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Chot pahuncha raha/rahi hai.
*   **Injured (Verb/Adj):** Hurt or damaged.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Zakhmi ya jise chot lagi ho.
*   **Injury (Noun):** Physical damage or hurt.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Chot ya zakhm.
*   **Injuries (Noun):** More than one injury.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Aik se zyada chotein ya zakhm.
*   **Uninjured (Adj):** Not hurt, safe.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Bilkul sahi salamat, jise koi chot na lagi ho.

---

### 3. SYNONYMS

1.  **Harm:** (Nuqsan dena)
2.  **Damage:** (Kharaab karna ya nuqsan pahunchana)
3.  **Wound:** (Gehra zakhm)

---

### 4. ACADEMIC EXAMPLES

*   **Injure:** High voltage can **injure** the computer hardware. (High voltage computer hardware ko nuqsan pahuncha sakti hai.)
*   **Injures:** Careless handling **injures** the sensitive components. (La-parwahi se sensitive parts kharaab ho jate hain.)
*   **Injuring:** The virus is **injuring** the system files. (Virus system files ko nuqsan pahuncha raha hai.)
*   **Injured:** The **injured** user reported a screen defect. (Zakhmi user ne screen defect ki report ki.)
*   **Injury:** Eye **injury** is common in bad lighting. (Galat roshni ki wajah se aankhon ki chot aam hai.)
*   **Injuries:** Multiple **injuries** were reported in the lab accident. (Lab accident mein kai chotein aayi.)
*   **Uninjured:** The server remained **uninjured** during the power surge. (Power surge ke doran server sahi salamat raha.)

### 5. MIXED ACADEMIC SENTENCES

1. Static electricity can **injure** delicate microchips. (Static electricity delicate chips ko nuqsan de sakti hai.)
2. Please avoid **injuring** the cables during setup. (Setup ke doran cables ko kharaab hone se bachayen.)
3. The technician was **injured** while moving the heavy server. (Heavy server uthate waqt technician zakhmi ho gaya.)
4. Data loss is a major **injury** to a research project. (Data ka zaya hona research project ke liye bara nuqsan hai.)
5. Luckily, the hardware was **uninjured** after the drop. (Khushqismati se, girne ke baad hardware ko kuch nahi hua.)
6. Frequent screen use **injures** the eyes. (Zyada screen use karne se aankhon ko nuqsan hota hai.)
7. Many minor **injuries** occur in crowded labs. (Bhari lab mein aksar choti-moti chotein lag jati hain.)
8. The software prevents **injuring** the database files. (Software database files ko nuqsan se bachata hai.)
9. He remained **uninjured** despite the equipment failure. (Equipment kharaab hone ke bawajood woh sahi salamat raha.)
10. Proper training prevents **injuring** oneself in the lab. (Sahi training lab mein khud ko chot se bachati hai.)
11. The report lists all **injuries** related to the device. (Report device se mutaliq tamam zakhmon ki fehrist deti hai.)
12. Never **injure** the motherboard with metal tools. (Metal tools se motherboard ko kabhi nuqsan na den.)
13. The **injured** sensor was replaced immediately. (Kharaab sensor ko foran badal diya gaya.)
14. The system is **uninjured** and running perfectly. (System sahi salamat hai aur behtareen chal raha hai.)
15. Avoiding **injuries** is a priority for lab safety. (Lab safety mein choton se bachna pehli tarjeeh hai.)
