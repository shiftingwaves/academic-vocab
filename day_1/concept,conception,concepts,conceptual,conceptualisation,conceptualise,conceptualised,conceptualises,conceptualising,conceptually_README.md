### 2. EXPLANATION

*   **Concept (Noun):** An idea or a basic principle. | *Ek idea ya kisi cheez ka bunyadi khayal.*
*   **Concepts (Noun):** More than one idea. | *Ek se zyada khayalat ya ideas.*
*   **Conception (Noun):** The start of an idea; the way you understand something. | *Kisi idea ka shuru hona ya kisi cheez ko samajhne ka tareeqa.*
*   **Conceptualise (Verb):** To form an idea or plan in your mind. | *Apne zehen mein koi idea ya plan banana.*
*   **Conceptualises (Verb):** (He/She/It) forms an idea. | *Woh idea banata hai.*
*   **Conceptualised (Verb):** Formed an idea in the past. | *Past mein koi idea banaya.*
*   **Conceptualising (Verb):** The process of forming an idea. | *Idea banane ka amal.*
*   **Conceptualisation (Noun):** The act of forming an idea. | *Idea banane ka process.*
*   **Conceptual (Adjective):** Related to ideas, not physical things. | *Jo sirf zehen mein ho, physically na ho.*
*   **Conceptually (Adverb):** In a way that relates to ideas. | *Idea ke lehaz se.*

### 4. ACADEMIC EXAMPLES

*   **Concept:**
    1. The concept of cloud computing is very popular today. (Cloud computing ka concept aaj kal bohat mashhoor hai.)
    2. We must understand the core concept of this algorithm. (Hamein is algorithm ka bunyadi concept samajhna hoga.)
*   **Conception:**
    1. His conception of the software design was very unique. (Uska software design ka tasawwur/soch bohat alag thi.)
    2. The project was difficult from its very conception. (Yeh project shuru se hi mushkil tha.)
*   **Conceptualise:**
    1. It is hard to conceptualise how AI learns so fast. (Yeh samajhna ya zehen mein lana mushkil hai ke AI itni tezi se kaise seekhta hai.)
    2. Designers conceptualise the user interface before coding. (Designers coding se pehle user interface ka khayal zehen mein banate hain.)
*   **Conceptual:**
    1. There are many conceptual problems with this model. (Is model ke sath bohat se zehni/theoretical maslay hain.)
    2. The research focuses on conceptual changes in networking. (Yeh research networking mein hone wali bunyadi tabdeelion par hai.)
*   **Conceptually:**
    1. The two systems are conceptually different. (Yeh dono systems soch ke lehaz se mukhtalif hain.)
    2. Conceptually, the program is simple but hard to build. (Soch ke lehaz se program asaan hai, lekin banana mushkil hai.)

### 5. MIXED ACADEMIC SENTENCES

1. The **concept** of big data is changing research. (Big data ka concept research ko badal raha hai.)
2. Scholars **conceptualise** new ways to protect data. (Scholars data ko protect karne ke naye tareeqay sochte hain.)
3. We **conceptualised** the mobile app last month. (Hum ne pichle mahine mobile app ka khayal banaya tha.)
4. **Conceptual** errors can crash the whole program. (Soch ki ghaltiyan poore program ko crash kar sakti hain.)
5. **Conceptually**, the code works, but it is slow. (Soch ke lehaz se code theek hai, magar slow hai.)
6. The **conceptualisation** of this AI model took a year. (Is AI model ko sochnay aur plan karne mein ek saal laga.)
7. Many **concepts** in physics are used in coding. (Physics ke bohat se concepts coding mein use hote hain.)
8. The **conception** of the internet changed the world. (Internet ka tasawwur duniya badal gaya.)
9. She is **conceptualising** a new game engine. (Woh ek naya game engine soch rahi hai.)
10. The teacher **conceptualises** the lesson clearly. (Teacher lesson ko achi tarah zehen mein bithate hain.)
11. Are these two **concepts** related? (Kya yeh dono concepts aapas mein jure hue hain?)
12. The team **conceptualised** a secure database. (Team ne ek secure database ka plan banaya.)
13. **Conceptual** clarity is important for students. (Students ke liye soch mein wazahat hona zaroori hai.)
14. It is **conceptually** impossible to predict every error. (Har ghalti ko pehle se sochna namumkin hai.)
15. They finished the **conceptualisation** phase today. (Unhon ne aaj planning ka phase khatam kar diya.)
