### 1. FULL WORD FAMILY

*   **Verb forms**
    *   Culture (less common as a verb, means to grow cells in a lab)
*   **Noun forms**
    *   Culture (base form)
    *   Cultures (plural form)
*   **Adjective forms**
    *   Cultural
    *   Cultured
    *   Uncultured
*   **Adverb forms**
    *   Culturally

---

### 2. EXPLANATION

*   **Culture (Noun):** The way of life of a group of people.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Logon ka rehne sehne ka tareeqa ya unki society.
*   **Cultures (Noun):** More than one type of culture.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Ek se zyada mukhtalif societies ya rehne sehne ke tareeqay.
*   **Cultural (Adjective):** Related to culture.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Culture se taluq rakhne wali cheez.
*   **Cultured (Adjective):** Someone who knows a lot about art, music, and manners.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Aisa shakhs jo tameez-daar ho aur jise art ya education ki samajh ho.
*   **Uncultured (Adjective):** Someone who does not have good manners or knowledge of arts.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Jisay tameez na ho ya jo bilkul gawar ho.
*   **Culturally (Adverb):** In a way that relates to culture.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Culture ke lihaz se.
*   **Culture (Verb - Scientific):** To grow bacteria or cells in a lab.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Lab mein bacteria ya cells ko grow karna.

### 4. ACADEMIC EXAMPLES

*   **Culture:** 
    1. The company culture encourages developers to share their code. (Company ka culture developers ko code share karne ki himmat deta hai.)
    2. We studied the digital culture of young students. (Hum ne young students ke digital culture ka mutalia kiya.)
*   **Cultures:**
    1. Different cultures use technology in different ways. (Mukhtalif cultures technology ko mukhtalif tareeqon se use karte hain.)
    2. The research compared two distinct cultures. (Research ne do mukhtalif cultures ka mawazna kiya.)
*   **Cultural:**
    1. There are many cultural differences in how people use social media. (Social media use karne ke tareeqon mein bohat se cultural farq hain.)
    2. Language is a key part of our cultural identity. (Zaban hamari cultural identity ka ehem hissa hai.)
*   **Cultured:**
    1. The software was tested on a cultured sample of bacteria. (Software ko bacteria ke cultured sample par test kiya gaya.)
*   **Uncultured:**
    1. It is wrong to label any group as uncultured. (Kisi bhi group ko uncultured kehna ghalat hai.)
*   **Culturally:**
    1. The app was culturally adapted for users in Pakistan. (App ko Pakistan ke users ke liye culturally adjust kiya gaya.)

### 5. MIXED ACADEMIC SENTENCES

1. The **culture** of the IT department is very innovative. (IT department ka culture bohat innovative hai.)
2. We must respect all **cultures** in the workplace. (Hamein workplace mein tamam cultures ki izzat karni chahiye.)
3. **Cultural** values affect how we design websites. (Cultural values asar dalte hain ke hum websites kaise design karte hain.)
4. The lab is trying to **culture** the new virus sample. (Lab naye virus sample ko culture karne ki koshish kar rahi hai.)
5. This website is **culturally** sensitive to local users. (Yeh website local users ke liye culturally sensitive hai.)
6. Being **cultured** helps in international business meetings. (Cultured hona international business meetings mein madad deta hai.)
7. The study of different **cultures** helps in global software design. (Mukhtalif cultures ki study global software design mein madad deti hai.)
8. **Cultural** exchange is important for modern science. (Cultural exchange jadeed science ke liye zaroori hai.)
9. Digital **culture** is changing very fast. (Digital culture bohat tezi se badal raha hai.)
10. The **cultured** bacteria showed growth after 24 hours. (Cultured bacteria ne 24 ghanton baad growth dikhayi.)
11. Many people think it is **uncultured** to use phones during meetings. (Bo bohat se log meetings mein phone use karna uncultured samajhte hain.)
12. **Culturally** speaking, the team is very diverse. (Culturally dekha jaye to team bohat diverse hai.)
13. We need to preserve our **cultural** heritage in digital form. (Hamein apne cultural heritage ko digital form mein mehfooz karna chahiye.)
14. The **culture** of open-source software is very helpful. (Open-source software ka culture bohat madadgar hai.)
15. Does the software respect the **cultural** norms of the user? (Kya software user ke cultural norms ka khayal rakhta hai?)

### 6. CSAVL REVISION SHEET

| Word | Simple Meaning | Academic Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Culture** | Way of life / Lab growth | Used for workplace or biology |
| **Cultural** | Related to society | Used for social research |
| **Culturally** | In a social way | Used for adapting products |
| **Cultured** | Refined / Grown in lab | Used for cells or people |

**Memory Trick:** Think of **"Culture"** as the "roots" of a person. Just like a plant needs roots to grow, a person needs their **culture** to understand their identity. If you are **cultured**, you have "grown" your mind!
