Hello! I am your English instructor. Today, we will learn about the word **"Principle"** and its family. These words are very important in academic writing and computer science, especially when talking about ethics or the rules of how things work.

Let’s start!

---

### 1. FULL WORD FAMILY

*   **Verb forms:** None (This family does not have a common verb form).
*   **Noun forms:**
    *   Principle (singular)
    *   Principles (plural)
*   **Adjective forms:**
    *   Principled
    *   Unprincipled

---

### 2. EXPLANATION

*   **Principle (Noun):** A basic rule or belief that controls how something works.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Yeh koi bunyadi usool ya rule hota hai jis par koi cheez chalti hai.
*   **Principles (Noun):** More than one basic rule.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Ek se zyada usool ya rules.
*   **Principled (Adjective):** Someone who has strong moral rules or behaves honestly.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Aisa shakhs jo apne usoolon ka pakka ho aur imandar ho.
*   **Unprincipled (Adjective):** Someone who does not have good moral rules or acts dishonestly.
    *   *Roman Urdu:* Aisa shakhs jis mein koi achay usool na hon ya jo bura kaam kare.

---

### 3. SYNONYMS

*   **Principle:**
    1. **Rule:** Koi usool ya qanoon.
    2. **Foundation:** Kisi cheez ki bunyad.
    3. **Standard:** Koi mayar ya level.

---

### 4. ACADEMIC EXAMPLES

*   **Principle:**
    1. The core **principle** of computer security is data protection. (Computer security ka bunyadi usool data ko mehfooz rakhna hai.)
    2. We must follow the **principle** of logic in programming. (Hamein programming mein logic ke usoolon ki pairwi karni chahiye.)
*   **Principles:**
    1. Design **principles** help developers create better software. (Design ke usool developers ki behtar software banane mein madad karte hain.)
    2. These scientific **principles** explain how the CPU works. (Yeh scienci usool batate hain ke CPU kaise kaam karta hai.)
*   **Principled:**
    1. A **principled** researcher always reports true data. (Ek usool pasand researcher hamesha sacha data report karta hai.)
    2. The team made a **principled** decision to stop the project. (Team ne ek imandari wala faisla kiya ke project rok diya jaye.)
*   **Unprincipled:**
    1. **Unprincipled** hackers steal private information. (Ghair-usooli hackers private maloomat chura lete hain.)
    2. He was fired for his **unprincipled** behavior at work. (Use daftar mein ghair-akhlaqi rawayye ki wajah se nikal diya gaya.)

### 5. MIXED ACADEMIC SENTENCES

1. Algorithms are based on mathematical **principles**. (Algorithms riyazi ke usoolon par mabni hote hain.)
2. It is important to be a **principled** software engineer. (Ek usool pasand software engineer banna zaroori hai.)
3. **Unprincipled** companies often ignore user privacy. (Ghair-usooli companiyan aksar user ki privacy ko nazar-andaz karti hain.)
4. The first **principle** of networking is connectivity. (Networking ka pehla usool connectivity hai.)
5. Scientists follow strict **principles** during experiments. (Scientists tajurbat ke dauran sakht usoolon ki pairwi karte hain.)
6. A **principled** leader always values transparency. (Ek usool pasand leader hamesha transparency ko ehmiyat deta hai.)
7. Using **unprincipled** methods to gain data is wrong. (Data hasil karne ke liye ghair-usooli tareeqe istemal karna galat hai.)
8. The project follows the **principle** of modularity. (Yeh project modularity ke usool par chalta hai.)
9. Many ethical **principles** apply to Artificial Intelligence. (AI par bahut se akhlaqi usool laagu hote hain.)
10. He is a **principled** man who never cheats in research. (Woh ek usool pasand admi hai jo research mein kabhi dhoka nahi deta.)
11. **Unprincipled** developers create malware to harm others. (Ghair-usooli developers dusron ko nuksan pohanchane ke liye malware banate hain.)
12. Understanding the **principles** of coding is essential. (Coding ke usoolon ko samajhna zaroori hai.)
13. The **principle** of least privilege protects the database. (Database ko mehfooz rakhne ke liye 'least privilege' ka usool istemal hota hai.)
14. An **unprincipled** action can destroy a researcher's career. (Ek ghair-usooli harkat researcher ka career tabah kar sakti hai.)
15. We must maintain high **principles** in our academic work. (Hamein apne academic kaam mein unche usool qaim rakhne chahiye.)

### 6. CSAVL REVISION SHEET

**Core Meaning:** Rules, beliefs, or moral behavior.

| Word | Simple Meaning | Academic Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Principle** | A rule or belief | Used for laws of science/logic. |
| **Principles** | Rules (plural) | Used for sets of guidelines. |
| **Principled** | Honest/Moral | Used for ethical people/decisions. |
| **Unprincipled** | Dishonest/Bad | Used for unethical actions. |

**Memory Trick:**
*   **Principle** ends in **"-ple"**. Think: **P**-**L**-**E** = **P**roper **L**ogical **E**xplanation.
*   **Principled** is about a **Person** (starts with P) who has rules.
